Foodiegraph App Tutorial (Original Proposal)

  • Step 1
    Upload
    The main function of our app includes uploading the picture of your available ingredients (or choice bars). The app can then search on the web to retrieve verified recipes containing these foods, filters containing choices “my materials only” and “my materials+”.
  • Step 2
    My Materials Filter
    If the user chooses “my materials only” choice, then the app would only explore recipes using only the materials selected previously or uploaded. If the user chooses “my materials+” choice then the app would automatically search for results including but not restricted to the ingredients that were previously selected or uploaded.
  • Step 3
    Flashcards
    All our recipes come in easy bits fed to users via flashcards. With the recipes chunked down into different slides, users could have a better time digesting the instructions and knowledge each step explicitly better and easier. Demonstration pictures would be included on each slide. Users can mark a recipe as one’s favorite and/or add to one’s library. The annotation feature would be available for users to mark on the recipes freely, including but not limiting to the purpose of noting where to pay extra attention and how to control the fire intensity.
  • Step 4
    Customization
    There are also secondary functions to aid the users through their journey of exploring their cooking choices. Some of them include the timer function, which would provide a timer, like it is named. The users could use the timer to get the right time and not overcook the food by accident. The stores collaboration feature is a map feature that includes a list and/or updates of local supermarkets and places to get materials. The “My Recipe” function can provide variations of the food recipe and perhaps even upload one’s own in one’s library swiftly via the form of flashcards. The friend feature of our app also enables the sharing of customized recipes! If one wants to taste the food of one’s home country in foreign lands, this app provides a “Search By Nation” function that enlists foods of different origins.
  • Step 5
    Your Preferences
    Moreover, our search engine would be AI-based and would adapt to the styles of cuisine to sort out different sections that one might possibly like. By collecting the data of the user’s saved and favorite recipes, the app would be able to put what might be the most relevant to the user’s preferences into the “recommended” part.
  • Step 6
    Contribute
    In addition, users are welcome to be a contributor to the app by uploading original recipes. Our app will include a separate section for users to share their own recipes and culinary tips. Users can also draw inspiration from contents uploaded by other users. This gives users access to various cooking techniques and recipes for unique cuisine choices of different countries.
